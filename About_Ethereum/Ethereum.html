Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain with smart contract functionality.

Advantages of Ethereum

Aside from decentralized and anonymity, Ethereum also has various other benefits, such as a lack of censorship.

Ethereum has smart contracts where rules are hard-coded into the contract & cannot be tempered with by either party once written.

Ethereum is much safer than a simple server that someone with ill intensions would need to control 51% of the network to make a change, it's nearly impossible in most cases.

Disadvantages of Ethereum

Even if it sounds like the perfect plateform, Ethereum has a few key issues that need to be worked out.

1. The first is Scalabilty:

Due to the Proof-of-work consensus algorithm, Ethereum is limited by block validation times,(slow transaction speed) and high gas fees.

2. The Second is Accessibility :

Ethereum is expensive to develop on and challenging to intract with for users unfamiliar with its technology. Some plateforms require specific wallets

History of Ethereum

Ethereum was conceived in 2013 by programmer Vitalik Buterin.

Additional founders of Ethereum included Gavin Wood, Charles Hokinson, Anthony Di lario and Joseph Lubin.

In 2014, development work began and was crowdfunded, and the network went live on 30 july 2015.
