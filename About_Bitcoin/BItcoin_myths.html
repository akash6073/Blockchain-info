Myth #1:

Bitcoin is a bubble

Bubbles are economic cycles characterized by unsustainable rise in market value. They eventually pop when investors realixe prices are much higher than an asset's fundamental value.

Real Story:

Bitcoin has gone through multiple price cycles over the course of over 12 years & has recovered each time to new all new highs.

Myth #2:

Bitcoin has no real-wolrd uses

Critics like to claim that Bitcoin isn't usefull in real world - or if it does have a use, it's mostly usefull for illicit activity Neither of those statements are true.

Real Story:

Bitcoin has a long history of making payments to anyone in the world, all without a bank or payment processor in middle.

Myth #3:

Bitcoin doesn't have real value

While Bitcoin might not be backed by a physical asset like gold, neither is US dollar or virtually any modern fiat currency.

Real Story:

Bitcoin is hard-coded to be scarce, which helps make it resistant to inflation. Inflation with fiat currencies can occure when large quantities are created, thus diluting the existing supply.

Myth #4:

Bitcoin will be replaced by a competitor

Real Story:

Bitcoin was the first really successful digital money.

And while new cryptocurrencies have long promised to overtake Bitcoin via new features or other advantages, none have cone close.

Myth #5:

Investing in Bitcoin is gambling

Bitcoin has experienced significant price volatility over the last decade, that's to be expected of a young and growing market.

Real Story:

After Bitcoin's genesis block in 2010, it has steadily gained long-term value. And as Bitcoin has continued to mature, a robust regulatory structure in countries arround the world has helped to attract a wave of institutional investments (Tesla,hedge funds).

Myth #6:

Bitcoin is bad for the environment.

Bitcoin mining is an energy-intensive process. But determinig the environmental impact is hard. For one thing, all aspects of the digital economy require energy.

Real Story:

Bitcoin is more efficient than traditional banking & gold mining on a global scale. Also significant portion of Bitcoin mining is powered by renewable-energy sources (including wind, hydro, and solar).

Myth #7:

Bitcoin isn't secure

Real Story:

The Bitcoin network has never been hacked its open-source code has been scrutinized by countless security experts and computer scientist.

Bitcoin was also the first digital currency to solve the doube-spend problem, making "trustless" peer-to-peer currencies a reality.

Also, All Bitcoin transaction are irreversible.
